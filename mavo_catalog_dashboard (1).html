🔑
The Key Insight: Retention Beats Raw Numbers
Mofe had 10× more daily streams at its peak than Tumo Weto — but Tumo Weto has 3× more total streams. This is what retention looks like in practice. A song that streams at 100K/day for 6 months earns more than a song that hits 1M/day for 2 weeks. The money is in the long tail, not the spike.

🎯 Strategic Takeaways
🌱
Slow-Burn = Long-Term Income
Songs like Tumo Weto and No keep generating revenue long after release. Every new release should aim to build this type of listener loyalty, not just rack up first-week numbers.
⚡
Fast Spikes = Short Bursts
Mofe's massive opening peak was real income — but it came and went quickly. Spike songs are valuable for visibility and playlist placements, but they need retention-focused follow-ups to lock in new listeners.
🏛️
Stable Catalog = Predictable Passive Income
Escaladizzy 2 has been generating steady income for almost a year. Building more songs like this is how music becomes a business asset rather than a one-time event.
🌍
US/UK Streams Pay More
The same 1 million streams pay 4–5× more when they come from the US or UK versus Nigeria. Growing international audience reach — through features, playlist pitching, or targeted marketing — is the highest ROI growth strategy available.

🧠 Catalog Intelligence Summary

What the data says about where Mavo is — and where the opportunity is.

🏗️
Mixed Performance Architecture
The catalog contains all the archetypes of a healthy streaming business: a slow-burn anchor (Tumo Weto), a mature passive asset (Escaladizzy 2), a viral hit (Mofe), a balanced grower (Aura Salad), and a deep catalog stabilizer (No). This diversity is a strength.
🎯
Consistent Audience Reach
Nigeria (41–60%), US (10–16%), South Africa (4–9%) — this pattern holds across every release, every format, every year. Mavo has a defined, loyal fanbase. The consistency makes planning easier and audience growth more predictable.
⚡
Retention Drives Everything
The biggest variation between songs isn't peak size — it's how long people keep listening. Songs with low decay (Tumo Weto, No, Escaladizzy 2) generate far more total revenue than their peak numbers suggest. Retention is the metric that matters most.
💎
Long-Tail Assets Already Exist
Most artists don't build genuine catalog assets until well into their career. Mavo already has two. Escaladizzy 2 and No are still streaming regularly — that's passive income with no additional marketing spend. Protect and build on these.
🚀
Cross-Platform Gap Is an Opportunity
3 of 5 songs have zero YouTube presence. Tumo Weto has 3.16M YouTube views alongside its Spotify performance — and earns more as a result. Activating YouTube on the back catalog could add meaningful additional revenue without any new music.
📈
Strong Optimization Potential
With consistent audience geography, multiple catalog archetypes, and two proven long-tail assets, the framework is in place. The next phase is optimizing: more retention-focused releases, YouTube activation, and growing high-yield markets (US, UK) to maximize revenue per stream.
🎵
The Bottom Line
Mavo's catalog already generates consistent passive income from multiple asset types. The foundation is stronger than most artists at this stage. The biggest opportunity is not more songs — it's activating the existing catalog more aggressively across platforms and markets, while continuing to release songs built for retention, not just reach.
